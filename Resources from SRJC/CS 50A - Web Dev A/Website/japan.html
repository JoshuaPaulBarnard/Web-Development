Himeji Castle

Himeji Castle is situated in the city of Himeji, home to around 550,000 people. Himeji is inbetween Okayama and Kobe along the Great Inland Sea of Japan. Himeji Castle is considered one of the most beautiful and worthwhile castles to visit in all of Japan. It is one of only twelve original castles left in Japan, and survived the bombardments from American forces during World War 2 purely by chance.

Japans Landscape

The landscape of Japan is very beautiful with lush greenery and many mountains. The country of Japan has comparable landmass to California. Where California is wider, Japan is longer giving it great amounts of coastline. As such the Japanese people have always relied on fishing as a major source for food.

This picture was taken outside of Fukiya Furusato, a edo period style village.

Kosan-Ji Temple

Kosan-Ji Temple is a spectacularly beautiful Buddhist Temple on Ikuchijima. Ikuchijima is a small island within the Great Inland Sea of Japan, between the main island of Honshu and the middle island of Shikoku. Jima and Shima mean island, so Ikuchijima simply means Ikuchi Island.

Wikipedia's Definition of Japan

Japan (Japanese: 日本 Nippon [nip̚põ̞ɴ] or Nihon [nihõ̞ɴ]; formally 日本国 About this sound Nippon-koku or Nihon-koku, "State of Japan") is an island country in East Asia. Located in the Pacific Ocean, it lies to the east of the Sea of Japan, the East China Sea, China, Korea and Russia, stretching from the Sea of Okhotsk in the north to the East China Sea and Taiwan in the south. The kanji that make up Japan's name mean "sun origin", and it is often called the "Land of the Rising Sun".
